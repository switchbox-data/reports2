## II. PURPOSE OF TESTIMONY

**Q.** **On whose behalf are you testifying?**

**A.** I am testifying on behalf of the People of the State of Illinois, represented by the Office of the Attorney General ("AG").

**Q.** **What is the purpose of your testimony?**

**A.** On February 6, 2024, The Peoples Gas Light and Coke Company ("Peoples" or "the Company") filed Direct Testimony on Rehearing in this case. Included with that Direct Testimony is a quantification of the revenue requirement associated with two general categories of work that the Company is seeking to include in its test year rate base: 1) emergency, safety, and reliability ("ESR") work and 2) work in progress that was started in 2023 (together, "incremental plant"). AG Witnesses Bill McAleb, Jim Crowley, and Jeremy Walker (collectively, the "AG Gas Technical Panel") have proposed certain adjustments to the projects that the Company is proposing to include in rate base. The purpose of this testimony is to quantify the additional revenue requirement of the incremental plant net of the adjustments proposed by the AG Gas Technical Panel and address the impact of the additional proposed spending and revenue requirement on the Company.

## III. REVENUE REQUIREMENT OF INCREMENTAL PLANT

**Q.** **What additional revenue requirement did the Company calculate for the incremental plant that it is seeking to include in rate base?**

**A.** In its Amendatory Order of January 3, 2024, the Commission found a total revenue requirement of $1.005 billion and authorized a revenue increase of $304.6 million. The Company has calculated an additional revenue requirement of $7.9 million in its Rehearing testimony. This represents an increase of approximately 0.8% over the revenue requirement found by the Commission in its Amendatory Order.

**Q.** **What additional revenue requirement have you calculated for the incremental plant net of the adjustments proposed by the AG Gas Technical Panel?**

**A.** I have calculated an additional revenue requirement of $1.6 million. The calculation of the additional revenue requirement is shown on AG Exhibit 9.01, Schedule 1P accompanying this testimony.

**Q.** **Can you explain how you have calculated this additional revenue requirement?**

**A.** Yes. The additional revenue requirement associated with the incremental plant additions consists in general of two components: the return on the net increase in rate base and depreciation expense. The return component is calculated by applying the authorized rate of return to the net increase in rate base. Depreciation expense is calculated by applying the authorized depreciation accrual rate to the net increase in plant in service.

**Q.** **How did you calculate the net increase in rate base associated with the incremental plant additions?**

**A.** The calculation of the net rate base begins with the additions to plant in service. The test year rate base reflects the average of plant as of the beginning of the year and the end of the year. Therefore, for any category of plant additions in a given year, the average will be the balance as of the beginning of the year (zero) and the balance as of the end of the year (the amount of the plant additions). This is simply calculated by dividing the amount of the plant additions by two.

To calculate the increase in net rate base, the increase to plant in service is reduced by the increments to the balances of accumulated reserve for depreciation and accumulated deferred income taxes ("ADIT").

**Q.** **How did you calculate the increment to the depreciation reserve?**

**A.** As described below, depreciation expense is calculated by applying the authorized depreciation accrual rate to the average balance of plant in service. The offset for accumulated depreciation is the average of the balance at the beginning of the year (zero) and the end of the year (the expense recorded in the year).

**Q.** **What does the balance of ADIT represent?**

**A.** The Company uses accelerated depreciation for income tax purposes and records deferred income taxes on the difference between tax and book depreciation. The balance of ADIT represents the cumulative effect of deferred income tax expense recorded by the Company and included in its revenue requirement but not yet actually paid.

Applicable Internal Revenue Service regulations require that when a future test year is used to determine the revenue requirement for a rate regulated public utility, the rate base deduction for incremental ADIT must be calculated using a specified proration formula rather than simply averaging the beginning and ending balances. Assuming that book and tax depreciation accrue evenly over the year, the specified proration method results in an average ADIT balance of approximately 46.2% (rather than 50%) of the deferred income tax expense for the year.

**Q.** **How did you calculate the depreciation expense on the incremental plant?**

**A.** I calculated the depreciation expense by applying the approved depreciation accrual rate of 2.15% to the average balance of plant in service.

**Q.** **Can you explain what depreciation represents?**

**A.** Yes. Depreciation represents the return _of_ the Company's capital investment in its plant in service (as opposed to the return _on_ investment, or the cost of capital). The annual depreciation expense is calculated by multiplying the applicable depreciation rates by the plant in service included in each of the Company's plant accounts. Depreciation rates are typically developed by spreading the cost of the plant, including any salvage value or cost of removal, over its estimated useful life. The depreciation expense included in the Company's operating statement and revenue requirement is a non-cash operating expense, meaning that it does not entail a current outlay of funds as does, for example, labor expense.

The inclusion of depreciation expense in the Company's revenue requirement provides the Company with a source funds, as the Company recovers the depreciation expense in rates but does not have to actually disburse cash in payment of this expense. This source of funds in conjunction with retained earnings and other non-cash expenses included in the revenue requirement, such as amortization expense and deferred income tax expense, can be used to finance additions to utility plant in the test year and beyond. Thus, as new plant is added, the cost of embedded plant is being recovered in rates and is helping to fund the cost of the new plant that is being added.

**Q.** **Can you summarize how the increases in rate base and depreciation expense for the categories of work translate into your calculated additional revenue requirement?**

**A.** Yes. The detailed calculations supporting the rate base and depreciation expense for each individual category of work are shown on AG Exhibit 9.01, Schedule 5P, Pages 3-16.

The adjustment to rate base for each category of work carries forward to AG Exhibit 9.01, Schedule 4P, Pages 2 and 3. The sum of these adjustments carries forward to Schedule 3P and is included in the "Rehearing Adjustments" column. The pro forma rate base on Schedule 3P carries forward to Schedule 1P. The pro forma net operating divided by this rate base equals the Company's authorized rate of return.

The adjustment to depreciation expense for each category of work carries forward to AG Exhibit 9.01, Schedule 2P, Pages 2 and 3. Schedule 2P also reflects the derivative adjustments to income tax expenses related to the increase to depreciation expense. The effect of the rate base adjustments on the interest deduction for income taxes is included in the "Interest Synchronization" adjustment on Page 1. The sum of these adjustments carries forward to Schedule 1 and is included in the "Rehearing Adjustments" column.

I have calculated a revenue change of $306,166,000 on AG Exhibit 9.01, Schedule 1, Line 24. This is an increase of $1,560,000 over the revenue change $304,606,000 in the Amendatory Order of January 3, 2024. The incremental revenue of $1,560,000 represents an increase of 0.16% over the total revenue requirement of $1,004,826,000 in the Amendatory Order. I have also prepared AG Exhibit 9.02, which is a comparison of the additional revenue requirement calculated by the Company to the additional revenue requirement calculated by the AG.

**A.** Yes.
