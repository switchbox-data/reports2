# Utility Rate Design: How Mandatory Monthly Customer Fees Cause Disproportionate Harm

**Source**: ripuc_4770_direct_testimony_acadia_lebel.pdf (pages 78–79)
**Exhibit**: AC-ML-8, Docket No. 4770
**Author**: National Consumer Law Center
**Contact**: John Howat, jhowat@nclc.org, 617-542-8010
**Date**: 2015
**Region**: CT, ME, NH, RI, VT

---

This data brief presents 2009 Residential Energy Consumption Survey (RECS) data showing that mandatory monthly customer fees disproportionately harm low-income, minority, and elderly households because these groups consume less electricity. Higher fixed charges shift costs from high-usage to low-usage customers — groups that are disproportionately low-income, minority, and elderly.

## Median 2009 Residential Electricity Usage (kWh), by Income

| Household Income | Median Electricity Usage (kWh) |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------ |
| < $25,000        | 3,904                          |
| $25,000–$49,999  | 6,198                          |
| $50,000–$74,999  | 7,358                          |
| $75,000–$99,999  | 8,235                          |
| ≥ $100,000       | 9,957                          |

[DIAGRAM DESCRIPTION: Bar chart showing median 2009 residential electricity usage by income bracket for CT, ME, NH, RI, VT. Usage rises monotonically from 3,904 kWh for households earning less than $25,000 to 9,957 kWh for households earning $100,000 or more — a 2.6× difference. The lowest-income bracket consumes roughly 40% of what the highest-income bracket does.]

## Median 2009 Residential Electricity Usage (kWh), by Race/Ethnicity

| Household Race   | Median Electricity Usage (kWh) |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Asian            | 3,369                          |
| African American | 5,967                          |
| Caucasian        | 7,266                          |
| Latino           | 4,794                          |

[DIAGRAM DESCRIPTION: Bar chart showing median 2009 residential electricity usage by race/ethnicity for CT, ME, NH, RI, VT. Caucasian households have the highest median usage at 7,266 kWh, followed by African American (5,967), Latino (4,794), and Asian (3,369). Asian and Latino households consume roughly 46% and 66% respectively of what Caucasian households consume.]
