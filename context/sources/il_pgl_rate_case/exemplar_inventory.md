## Detailed entries

### 1. Bradley Cebulko — PIO Direct (capital spending & gas system planning)

- **File**: `icc_23-0069_direct_testimony_pio_cebulko.md`
- **Date**: 2023-05-09 · **Submission**: 337548-588147 · **Type**: Direct
- **Party**: Public Interest Organizations (PIO) — Environmental Law & Policy Center, Environmental Defense Fund, NRDC, Illinois PIRG
- **Witness**: Bradley Cebulko, Senior Manager, Strategen Consulting (Sacramento, CA); previously Senior Policy Advisor, Washington UTC
- **Length**: 118 pages
- **Sections**:
  - I. Witness Identification and Qualifications
  - II. Purpose of Testimony
  - III. Background to this Rate Case
  - IV. Transformation of the Gas Distribution Utility Sector (declining sales, shareholder dividend growth)
  - V. Capital Expenditures (SMP, MAOP Reconfirmation, NPAs, Line Extensions)
  - VI. Bill Impact Analysis
  - VII. Gas System Planning (other states' gas plans)
  - VIII. Performance-Based Regulation (metrics, MYRPs, PIMs, riders)
  - IX. Rate Design (fixed-charge harm to low-usage / low-income customers)
  - X. Conclusion
- **Exhibits**: PIO Ex. 1.0 (testimony); 1.1 (CV); 1.2 (compiled discovery responses)
- **TL;DR**: Argues PGL's aggressive capital plan (especially SMP and line extensions) is risky and unsustainable given declining gas sales; recommends cost controls, NPA analysis, gas planning requirements, performance metrics, and lower residential customer charges.
